Privacy Policy for CuyahogaLookup

Last Updated: January 3, 2026

Introduction

CuyahogaLookup ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application.

Information We Collect

Information You Provide

  • Search History: Your property searches are stored locally on your device to provide recent-search functionality. This data is never transmitted to our servers.
  • Favorites: Properties you mark as favorites are stored locally on your device.
  • Contacts (Optional): If you choose to save property-owner information to your contacts, we request access to your device's contacts. This is entirely optional and the data is stored only on your device.

Information Collected Automatically

  • Device Information: We may collect device type, operating-system version, and unique device identifiers for analytics purposes.
  • Usage Data: We collect anonymous usage statistics to improve the app, including which features are used most frequently.
  • Advertising Identifier: For non-premium users, we use Google AdMob to display advertisements. AdMob may collect your device's advertising identifier to serve relevant ads.

Information from Third Parties

  • Property Data: All property information displayed in the app comes from publicly available records provided by the Cuyahoga County Fiscal Officer. We do not collect or store this data on our servers.

How We Use Your Information

  • Provide and maintain the app functionality
  • Improve and optimize the app experience
  • Display advertisements (for non-premium users)
  • Process subscription purchases through Apple's App Store
  • Analyze usage patterns to enhance features

Third-Party Services

Google AdMob

For non-premium users, we use Google AdMob to display advertisements. AdMob may collect and use data as described in Google's Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

RevenueCat

We use RevenueCat to manage premium subscriptions. RevenueCat processes subscription purchases through Apple's App Store. See RevenueCat's privacy policy: https://www.revenuecat.com/privacy

Apple App Store

Subscription payments are processed through Apple's App Store. Apple's privacy practices are described at: https://www.apple.com/privacy

Data Storage and Security

  • All user data (searches, favorites) is stored locally on your device
  • We do not operate servers that store your personal data
  • Property information is fetched directly from Cuyahoga County's public API
  • We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your information

Your Choices

Advertising

You can limit ad tracking by adjusting your device settings: iOS → Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking.

Premium Subscription

Subscribing to CuyahogaLookup Premium removes all advertisements from the app.

Data Deletion

Since all personal data is stored locally on your device, you can delete it by clearing the app's data in your device settings or uninstalling the app.

Children's Privacy

CuyahogaLookup is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have collected information from a child under 13, please contact us.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy in the app and updating the "Last Updated" date.

Contact Us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us at:
Email: privacy@justreed.com

California Privacy Rights

California residents may have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). Since we do not sell personal information and store data locally on your device, most CCPA provisions do not apply. Contact us for more information.

International Users

CuyahogaLookup is operated from the United States and is intended for users in the United States. If you are located outside the United States, please be aware that information you provide may be transferred to and processed in the United States.
